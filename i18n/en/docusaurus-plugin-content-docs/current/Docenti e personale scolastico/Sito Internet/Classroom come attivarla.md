## Tutorial on creating virtual classes and how to invite students and activate the definitive Meet link.

## Creating a Virtual Class and Managing Google Meet

### **1. Creating the Class on Google Classroom:**

* **Access Google Classroom:** Use your teacher credentials to access the platform.
* **Create a new course:** Click on the "+" button and select "Create".
* **Enter details:** Name the course, choose a section (if necessary), and add a brief description.
* **Create the code:** Google Classroom will automatically generate a class code. Communicate it to students to allow them to enroll.

### **2. Planning Lessons on Google Calendar:**

* **Create an event:** Access your Google Calendar and create a new event for each lesson.
* **Enter details:** Indicate date, time, lesson title, and add a brief description.
* **Generate Meet link:** Activate the "Add Google Meet video conferencing" option. A unique meeting link will be automatically generated.

### **3. Sharing the Meet Link with Students:**

* **Copy the link:** Copy the video conferencing link generated in Google Calendar.
* **Share the link:**
  * **Google Classroom:** Paste the link in the "Stream" section of your class.
  * **Email:** Send an email to all students with the link.
  * **Other:** You can also share the link via other communication platforms used by your class.

### **4. Customizing the Meet Meeting:**

* **Meeting settings:** Before starting the meeting, you can customize it:
  * **Who can join:** You can decide if anyone with the link can join or if permission is required.
  * **Wait for host to connect:** You can make students wait until you connect.
  * **Chat:** You can enable or disable chat during the meeting.
  * **Presentations:** You can share your screen to present teaching materials.
  * **Recording:** You can record the meeting to review it later.

### **5. Starting the Meeting:**

* **Click on the link:** At the time the lesson starts, click on the video conferencing link in Google Calendar.
* **Admit students:** Accept student participation requests.

### **Useful Tips:**

* **Create a routine:** Establish a routine for your online lessons, for example, a fixed time for connection and a precise order of activities.
* **Use Meet features:** Experiment with different Meet features, such as reactions, presentations, virtual whiteboards, to make lessons more engaging.
* **Engage students:** Ask questions, create polls, and encourage active student participation.
* **Record lessons:** Record lessons to allow students to review them in case of absence or difficulty.
* **Technical assistance:** Ensure students have a stable internet connection and an adequate device.
