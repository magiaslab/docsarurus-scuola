## Complete Guide to Accessing Google Classroom

### **What you need:**
* **Device:** Computer, tablet, or smartphone connected to the internet.
* **Login credentials:** Username and password provided by the school.
* **Invitation or code:** Provided by your teacher to join a specific class.

### **Procedure:**

1. **Open the browser:**
   * Launch your favorite internet browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, etc.).

2. **Go to Classroom:**
   * In the address bar, type **classroom.google.com** and press Enter.

3. **Log in:**
   * Enter your credentials (username and password) provided by the school and click **Next**.

### **Join the class:**

* **Via invitation:**
   * If you received an **invitation link** via email, simply click on the link and follow the instructions.
   * If you received an **invitation code**, follow these steps:
     1. Once logged into Classroom, you might see a "Join a class" section or a "+" button.
     2. Click on the appropriate option and enter the **code** provided by your teacher.
     3. Click on **Join**.

* **Via search:**
   * If you know the **class name** or the **teacher's name**, you can try searching for it in the search bar at the top.

### **What you can do in Classroom:**
* **View courses:** You will find listed all the courses you are enrolled in.
* **Access materials:** You will find assignments, announcements, files, and other materials shared by your teachers.
* **Submit assignments:** You can submit your assignments directly on the platform.
* **Interact with classmates:** You can participate in discussions and collaborate with your classmates.

### **Useful tips:**
* **Check your inbox:** Regularly check your school email for any invitations or important communications.
* **Ask for help:** If you have difficulties, do not hesitate to ask your teacher or your school's support staff for help.

**Important:**
* **Privacy:** Make sure to always use a personal account to access Google Classroom and not to share your credentials with others.
* **Updates:** Google Classroom is updated regularly, so there might be small differences in the interface.
