## Guide to using Google Drive for students

### What is Google Drive?
Google Drive is a free online storage space that allows you to save, share, and access your files from any internet-connected device. It's like having a virtual hard drive always available.

### How to access Google Drive:

1. **Open the browser:**
   * Launch your favorite internet browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, etc.).

2. **Go to Drive:**
   * In the address bar, type **drive.google.com** and press Enter.

3. **Log in:**
   * Use the same Google account you use to access Google Classroom. Enter your username and password and click on **Sign in**.

### Basic operations on Google Drive:

#### **1. Upload files:**
* **Click on "New"** in the top left corner.
* **Select "File upload"** or **"Folder upload"**.
* Find the file or folder on your computer and select it.

#### **2. Create a new folder:**
* Click on "New" and then on "Folder".
* Name your folder and press Enter.

#### **3. Share a file or folder:**
* **Right-click** on the file or folder.
* Select "Share".
* Enter the email addresses of the people you want to share with and choose the type of access you want to grant (viewer, commenter, editor).

#### **4. Collaborate on a document:**
* **Open the document** you want to edit.
* **Invite other people** to collaborate with you.
* Edit the document simultaneously, seeing changes made by others in real-time.

#### **5. Search for a file:**
* Use the **search bar** at the top to quickly find your files. You can search by name, file type, or date.

#### **6. Organize files:**
* **Create folders** to organize your files by subject, project, or type.
* **Move files** between folders by dragging and dropping them.

### Additional tips:
* **Offline use:** Enable offline mode to access your files even when you are not connected to the internet.
* **Mobile app:** Download the Google Drive app on your smartphone or tablet to access your files wherever you are.
* **Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides:** Create and edit documents, spreadsheets, and presentations directly on Google Drive.

### Why use Google Drive?
* **Access from anywhere:** Access your files from any internet-connected device.
* **Collaboration:** Work on projects with your classmates in real-time.
* **Security:** Your files are protected and automatically saved.
* **Integration with other Google apps:** Use Google Drive with other Google Workspace apps like Gmail and Calendar.

**With Google Drive, you will always have all your study materials at hand, you can collaborate with your classmates and improve your organization.**
